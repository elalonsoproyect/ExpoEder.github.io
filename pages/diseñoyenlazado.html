Key Concepts of the Cause and Effect Diagram

1.A cause-effect diagram cannot identify a root cause; graphically presents the many causes that may contribute to the observed effect.
2.It is a visual representation of the factors that could contribute to an observed effect that is being examined.
3.The interrelationships between the possible causal factors are clearly shown. A causal factor can appear in several places on the diagram.
4.The interrelationships are generally qualitative and hypothetical.
5.Focuses the attention of all team members on the specific problem at hand in a structured and systematic way.
